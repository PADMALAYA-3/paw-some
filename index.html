PAW-SOME

Snake

I have a personal inclination towards snakes because of the spiritual significance that they carry. They remind us, as a part of their life practice, that it is important to shed our scales and let go of the old and unserving part of ourselves.

Calf

While the world loves the cow, for the obvious reason that it provides milk; I have a rather soft spot for the calves. They come across as exceptionally innocent and carefree. Their eyes attract me the most. I just love the fact how domesticated and non-harming they're.

Kitten

If there is one animal I am all hearts for, then they are the kittens. Reasons would fall short why one wouldn't love these fluffiest of creatures. They're lovely,cute and snuggle-worthy. So much so that, I have named my project by their paws.

Camel

Not everyone is designed to walk on rough terrains and sustain the dry weathers. The ones who are designed though are very special to me. Camels for this reason stand tall in my eyes and in the deserts. They're an embodiment of tolerance and adaptability.

Dog

There is hardly anyone who doesn't like a pup or a dog. These wiggly creatures are the most giving, endearing and loyal. No wonder they're humans best friend.

Elephant

These huge mammals are not just a visionary treat, but also very harmless. Their stature could easily earn them the king of the jungle, but these fuss-free creatures choose not to fight for dominance. This fact has my heart. They're extremely emotional and that makes them human-friendly.

Lion

While I admire the elephant's quality to not chase for domination, I equally like the lion's hunt. It just hunts in such a fashion, that it earns the title "king of the jungle for itself". I guess potential should demand power. That is exactly the case here.

Seagull

One single book that I read early in my life, had me fascinate and obsess over the Seagulls. On that, they're extremely good-looking. Their images under the blue sky of the Antarctic are just divine.

Swans

It is said that swans have a peculiar skill of distinguishing pearls from mud. This ability reflects their innate purity. It is said that swans cannot live without their partners and they always exist in pairs. They almost starve themselves to death, if one of them dies. Everything about the swan makes them my favorite.

Tiger

When I first learnt about the Bengal Tigers, is when I started having deep fascination for them. They come across as extremely royal. The fact that no two tigers have the same stripes on their body, makes me relate with them a lot. No two humans have the same fingerprint as well. Nature always gives us cues, that we all belong to her.

Horse

Every animal by far is liked by me, because of the peculiar traits that they possess. But white horse has a very special place both in my heart and mind for its mythological importance. In Hinduism, it is believed that the 10th avatar of Lord Vishnu, the "KALKI" avatar, would come riding on a white horse. Millions of devotees here in my land are awaiting his arrival. Interestingly, what I have recently found out is that, almost every major religion like Christianity, Islam and Judaism believe that the world saviour would come riding on a white horse. All of us are looking forward to this messiah. And here I am taming my white-horse mind, racing from here and there. My soul has been it's riding saint.

Pigeon

One of my vivid memories from my childhood Independence Day celebration, was the unfurling of the tricolor, the pride of singing the national anthem and at times when few people decide to fly a white pigeon. Since, time immemorial, white pigeons are considered harvestors of good luck and peace. That is exactly what I pray for, that after all is said and done, let "peace" remain!
